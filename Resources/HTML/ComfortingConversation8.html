The GREAT Response

The following process prescribed by God and often demonstrated throughout scripture, especially by David in the Psalms, will help us move from a distressed mindset into a calm relational mindset. We will have been transformed by the renewing of our mind.

What could we be grateful to God for in the midst of this situation?
  • Deliberate appreciation is when we offer a sacriﬁce of thanksgiving.
  • There is a progression by which we come into God's presence and into His throne of grace. We come boldly because of the blood of Jesus that has made us holy and blameless.
  • And yet we follow the protocol of heaven. We ﬁrst enter into God's gates with thanksgiving (see Psalm 100).
What is the name of the Lord that we can rejoice in?
  • That you know God and he has not forsaken you?
  • That when you order your thoughts and words around Him he promises you salvation?
  • We enter into God's courts with praise.
  • What name of the Lord could you rejoice in right now?
What scripture promises can we engraft and hold on to at this time?
  • When we receive His word humbly is saves our souls.
What should we cry out to God and ask Him for in this situation?
  • Ask in prayer so that you may receive.
  • When you cry out the Lord hears you and delivers you.
  • Ask what God what you should do.
How can we triumph over evil with good?
  • Do not be overcome by evil but overcome evil with good.
  • Do not repay evil for evil but repay evil with blessing so that you may receive a blessing yourself.
For advanced comforting training, encourage yourself in the Lord by learning to calm your own soul.
